## Arterial (arterial.org)

**Type:** 501(c)(3) nonprofit media platform
**Location:** Los Angeles, with operations in NYC and Asheville, NC
**Leadership:** Scott "Sourdough" Power — Founder, CEO

### Mission
Amplify art with stories that entertain, inspire, and inform the general public. Three core tenets:
1. Artists are vital to society
2. Artists drive the economy
3. Artists deserve media exposure

### Vision
Become the most trusted, preferred source of contemporary stories and content about human creativity and the arts in America. Democratize art access, demystify the art world, humanize artists.

### Brand Voice
- Draws from "the vast untapped, overlooked, and neglected mass of artistic expression not found in formal settings"
- Positions against stale institutions (museums, auction houses, white cube galleries)
- Accessible, inclusive, advocate-driven

### Content Formats
Film, television, podcasting, radio, publishing, activations/events

### Programs
- Fiscal sponsor for sub-brands (Not Real Art is fiscally sponsored under Arterial)
- Grant programs (2024: $12K grant for The Underbelly Project documentary, premiered Miami Art Week)
- **Arthouse** — unscripted TV series

### Relationship to Sub-Brands
Arterial is the parent 501(c)(3). Not Real Art and Artsville USA operate under its umbrella. Arterial provides the nonprofit infrastructure, fiscal sponsorship, and overarching mission. The sub-brands handle day-to-day content production for their respective audiences.

## Not Real Art (notrealart.com)

**Type:** Fiscally-sponsored project under Arterial
**Focus:** Artist advocacy, community building, democratizing art careers
**Leadership:**
- **Scott "Sourdough" Power** — Founder, Creator, Executive Producer (also co-founder of Crewest Studio)
- **Morgan Laurens** — Curator and Editor-in-Chief
- **Kirsten Bengtson-Lykoudis** — Co-Curator (also primary daily user of artwork-archive export utility)

### Mission
Advocate for artists and their success. Provide exposure, connection, and recognition. Move beyond stale institutions to build community.

### Recurring Content Series

| Series | Format | Description | Campaign Implications |
|--------|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| **First Fridays** | Monthly exhibitions | Online exhibitions program, monthly cadence. Artists submit via Artwork Archive rolling submissions. Curated by Kirsten + Morgan. | Exhibition campaign type. Scrape exhibition URL, extract artworks + artists, generate 6-month promo. |
| **Q+Art** | Interview series | In-depth interviews with artists. Conducted by Morgan Laurens. Launched April 2025 in partnership with Artsville USA. | Interview/artist profile campaign type. Ask Morgan for highlights before generating. |
| **Podcast Network** | Audio | Multiple shows: artist interviews, creative professionals (Joey Flores, Jonathan Neil, Chris Davies). | Podcast episode campaign type. Extract key quotes, guest info. |
| **Art World Horror Stories** | Open call series | Seeks real stories from working artists. Features like Mellyssa Diggs, Wendy Newman, Jacobina Oele. | Story/feature campaign type. |
| **Featured Artist Submissions** | Ongoing open call | Artists submit for potential featuring across the network. | Pipeline feeds into other campaign types. |
| **Remote** | Public art series | By Dear McCleary, focused on public art. Under the Not Real Art umbrella. | Specialized campaign type — location-based public art promotion. |

### Content Patterns
- Artist interviews and profiles (deep-dive)
- Community testimonials
- Practical arts business resources
- Exhibition promotion (First Fridays)
- Cross-platform promotion (features go to social, podcasts, partner platforms)

Active on Instagram, likely Facebook, Twitter/X, others. Specific handles TBD.

## Artsville USA (artsvilleusa.com)

**Type:** Part of Arterial family
**Focus:** Contemporary American arts and crafts, centered on Asheville, NC
**Tagline:** "A group of artists, art professionals + enthusiasts supporting the arts community in Asheville"

### Key People
- **Scott Power** — appears in podcast discussions
- **Louise** — podcast co-host
- **Sherry Masters** — arts insider, studio tours via Art Connections partnership

### Recurring Content Series

| Series | Format | Description | Campaign Implications |
|--------|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Artsville USA Podcast** | Audio | Relaxed, insightful discussions celebrating creativity and craftsmanship. Asheville focus. | Podcast episode campaign type. |
| **Q+Art** | Interview series | Shared with Not Real Art, launched April 2025. Showcases Asheville's creative community. | Same as NRA Q+Art — cross-brand series. |
| **Virtual Gallery** | Online exhibition | 15 artists per cycle, competitive selection, zero-commission sales, 3 months free marketing + mentoring. | Exhibition-like campaign type. |
| **Art Studio Tours** | In-person events | Partnership with Art Connections. Working studios: glass, ceramics, fiber, wood, craft furniture, painting, sculpture, mixed media, jewelry, bookmaking. | Event promotion campaign type. |
| **Biltmore Lake Imaginative Studio Stroll** | Local event | Features local painters. | Event campaign type. |
| **Helene Stories Hotline** | Community response | Post-crisis artist visibility initiative. | Timely/responsive campaign type. |

### Focus Areas (Craft Forms)
Painting, pottery, woodworking, sculpture, jewelry, glass, ceramics, fiber arts, craft furniture, mixed media, bookmaking

### Brand Voice
- More craft-focused and Asheville-local compared to Not Real Art's broader scope
- Conversational, celebration-focused ("relaxed, insightful discussion")
- Community-responsive (adapts to local needs, e.g., disaster recovery)
- Accessibility and community-driven cultural development

### Differences from Not Real Art
- **Geographic focus:** Artsville = Asheville/Western NC. Not Real Art = national/broader
- **Content focus:** Artsville = crafts, makers, studio practice. Not Real Art = contemporary art, artist advocacy
- **Tone:** Artsville = warmer, more casual, local community. Not Real Art = advocacy-driven, slightly more editorial

## Data Sources (from Artwork Archive project)

- **Artists table** — enriched profiles with AI-generated bios, social profiles, tags
- **Artworks table** — classified artworks with images, dimensions, medium, relevance hypothesis
- **Campaigns table** — exhibition metadata, dates, venues, partner orgs

This data is the primary source for First Fridays exhibition campaigns. The promo scheduler can pull from it.
